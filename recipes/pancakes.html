Pancakes

Description

Pancakes are a beloved breakfast staple made from a simple batter of flour, eggs, and milk. Often cooked on a hot griddle or frying pan, they transform into thin, flat cakes with a golden-brown finish. Depending on the style, they can be light and fluffy like American flapjacks or thin and delicate like French crepes. They are traditionally served in a warm stack, topped with sweet additions like maple syrup, butter, or fresh fruit. Around the world, variations include everything from South African "pannekoek" with cinnamon sugar to savoury Asian rice pancakes.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg, beaten

Steps

  1. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl. Make a well in the center, and pour in milk, oil, and egg. Mix until smooth
  2. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat. Pour or scoop batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake; cook until bubbles form and the edges are dry, 1 to 2 minutes. Flip and cook until browned on the other side. Repeat with remaining batter.
  3. Serve hot and enjoy!
